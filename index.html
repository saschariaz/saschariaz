My research is situated at the intersection of political behavior, comparative political economy, and applied statistics. I study causal drivers of increasing fragmentation in European societies, with a focus on immigration, xenophobia and inequality. Methodologically, I specialize in quasi-experimental research design.

Before coming to Oxford, I completed a PhD in Government (2022) and Masters in Statistics (2020) at Harvard University.
